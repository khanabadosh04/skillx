1.

Government Agencies: Reach out to your local municipality or environmental department to inquire about their food waste management programs or initiatives. They may be able to provide you with relevant contacts or information.

2.

Nonprofit Organizations: There are several nonprofit organizations working on food waste reduction and management. Examples include Food Recovery Network, Feeding America, and Waste and Resources Action Programme (WRAP). Visit their websites and look for contact information or inquiry forms.

3.

Food Banks and Food Rescue Organizations: Contact local food banks or food rescue organizations in your area. They often have programs focused on collecting and distributing surplus food. They may also have information on food waste management initiatives.

4.

Waste Management Companies: Companies specializing in waste management and recycling may have expertise or services related to food waste. Look for waste management companies in your region and reach out to them for more information.
5.
Research Institutions and Universities: Academic institutions often conduct research on food waste management and may have experts or programs in this field. Contact the relevant departments or research centers to inquire about their work and potential contacts. Remember to provide specific details about your purpose or area of interest when reaching out to these organizations. This will help them direct you to the appropriate contacts or resources.
